Audio Clip

Audio Clips are used by Audio Sources to represent the audio asset imported into Unity. Unity supports both Mono and Stereo audio assets.


The Audio Clip Inspector

Audio Clips just work. The only thing you should have to do with them is reference them from within Audio Sources.

Sound assets only have read-only properties. Some of these properties can be changed in the Import Settings

Importing Audio Assets

Unity supports both Compressed or Uncompressed Audio. In the editor, compressed audio is in Ogg Vorbis format. On the iPhone device, compressed audio is Apple Native. Any audio format can be converted into Ogg Vorbis, but only some formats can be converted into Apple Native compressed audio.

File Type Conversion

.AIFFImports as uncompressed audio for short sound effects. Incompatible with Apple Native compressed format.
.WAVImports as uncompressed audio for short sound effects. Incompatible with Apple Native compressed format.
.OGGNative compressed audio format, incompatible with the iPhone device.
.MP3Imports as Apple Native compressed format for longer music tracks.
.M4AImports as Apple Native compressed format for longer music tracks.

Import Settings

When you are importing an audio file, you can select its final format and choose to force it to stereo or mono channels. To access the Import Settings, select the Audio Clip in the Project View and choose Assets->Import Settings... from the menubar, or click the Settings button in the Project View header.

Using Positional Audio

Positional audio is the placement and volume of sounds across the speakers. It simulates distance and location of sounds by fading volume and panning across speakers. Unity supports Positional audio for mono Audio Clips. Stereo sounds are always at the volume and panning determined by the source asset. For this reason, mono Clips are best for sound effects, and stereo Clips are best for music.

There are special cases, like if you wanted music playing from two unique in-game speaker environment objects. The closer you move your character to the camera, the louder the music will get. This setup would require the music in mono format.

If you have an Audio Clip that is in stereo, but you want to use it positionally, change the Channels property to Force Mono in the Import Settings. This allows you to be very creative with how you use audio in your game.

When to use Uncompressed Audio

Uncompressed audio clips are best for short sound effects. You can have multiple uncompressed audio clips playing simultaneously on the iPhone device. The file sizes are larger than compressed audio, so be aware that using long uncompressed audio clips can contribute to your final built player size.

When to use Compressed Audio

Compressed Audio is perfect for background music. The iPhone device supports only one compressed audio clip playing at once. If the device is already playing one compressed clip and another starts to play, the already-playing clip will stop playback immediately. If your music and your sound effects are all compressed, you won't be able to hear sound effects layered above your background music. This is why it's ideal to use compressed audio for a long song, and uncompressed audio for short sound effects.

Hints

  • There is not much of a reason to import your own .ogg files. Create your sounds in AIFF or WAV format, then you can compress them if you want to right inside the Import Settings.
  • Stereo sounds are always played as-is. If you want to use attenuation and other 3D audio effects, force your Clip to be mono in the Import Settings.
