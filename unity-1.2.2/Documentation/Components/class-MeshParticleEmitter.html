Mesh Particle Emitter

Components > Particle systems > Mesh Particle Emitter

Emits particles from a mesh. Particles are spawned from the surface of the mesh, which can be necessary when you want to make your particles interact in a complex way with objects.

Properties

Property:Function:
Emit Is the particle active?
Min & Max Size The minimum & maximum size of the particles at the time when they are spawned.
Min & Max Energy The minimum & maximum lifetime of the particles, measured in seconds.
Min & Max Emission The minimum & maximum number of particles
World Velocity The starting speed of particles in world space.
Local Velocity The starting speed of particles, measured in the object's orientation.
Rnd Velocity A random amount that is added to the velocity.
Emitter Velocity Scale How much of the emitter's speed does the particles inherit?
Simulate In World Space If enabled, the particles don't move when the emitter moves. If false, when you move the emitter, the particles follow it around.
One Shot If enabled, the particle numbers specified by min & max emission is spawned all at once. If disabled, the particles are generated in a long stream.
Min & Max Normal Velocity How much are particles thrown away from the mesh
Interpolate Triangles If enabled, particles are spawned all over the mesh's surface. If disabled, particles are only spawned from the mesh's vertrices.
Systematic If enabled, particles are spawned in the order of the vertrices defined in the mesh. Although you seldom have direct control over vertex order in meshes, most 3D modelling applications have a very systematic setup when using primitives. It is important that the mesh contains no faces in order for this to work.

Details

Mesh Particle Emitters are used when you want more precise control over the spawn position & directions than the simpler Ellipsiod Particle Emitter gives you.

All velocities are added together.

Hints

  • Be careful of using many large particles. This can seriously hinder performance on low-level machines. Always try to use the minimum number of particles to attain an effect. Mesh Particle Emitters (MPEs) are good for making advanced effects like a flaming sword: Take a sword object, and add a fire-like particle system, using an MPE to make the particles be spawned from positions on the sword's surface.
  • MPEs can also be used to make glow from a lot of lamps placed in a scene. Simply make a mesh with one vertex in the center of each lamp, and build an MPE from that with a halo material. Great for evil sci-fi worlds.
